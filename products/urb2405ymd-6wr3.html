FAE Distributor's Review

The K78 series is one of our most popular problem-solvers. Engineers love it because it's a simple, drop-in replacement for inefficient LM7805 linear regulators. Frequently Asked Questions

Is this really a drop-in replacement for an LM7805?

Yes, it has the same 3-pin layout (Input, Ground, Output) and can be used in most circuits designed for an LM7805 without any PCB changes. However, as a switching regulator, it may require small input/output capacitors for stability, as detailed in the datasheet.

What is the main advantage over a linear regulator?

Efficiency. A linear regulator burns the excess voltage as heat. A K78 converts it. For example, converting 24V to 5V at 0.5A, an LM7805 would dissipate over 9.5 Watts of heat (requiring a large heatsink), while the K78 would dissipate less than 0.2 Watts.
